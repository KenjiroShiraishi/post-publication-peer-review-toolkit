# Prompt: Monthly Affiliation-Based Literature Screening

This prompt is designed to help readers identify recently published academic articles associated with a specific institution, organization, research group, or advocacy-related body.

The purpose is not to accuse authors or identify misconduct.

The purpose is to support responsible post-publication peer review by finding candidate articles for human review.

## Intended Use

Use this prompt when you want to screen recently published articles connected to:

- a university
- a research institute
- a hospital
- a non-profit organization
- an advocacy group
- a professional association
- a government-related research group
- a commercial or industry-linked organization
- a policy-related research network

This prompt is especially useful when the topic may involve clinical care, public health, commercial products, regulation, policy, advocacy, or vulnerable populations.

## Prompt

Find academic articles published within the last one month that include authors affiliated with the following institution, organization, research group, or related body:

**[INSERT INSTITUTION / ORGANIZATION NAME]**

Please search public academic sources where possible, including PubMed, Crossref, publisher websites, journal websites, and other publicly accessible scholarly databases.

For each article, create a table with the following columns:

- Title
- Authors
- Journal
- Publication date
- DOI or URL
- Article type
- Relevant author affiliation
- Topic
- Main claim or purpose
- Conflict of interest statement
- Funding statement
- Possible reason for human review
- Priority for human review: High / Medium / Low / Unclear

## Screening Focus

For each article, check whether human review may be useful because of:

- unclear conflict of interest disclosure
- unclear funding statement
- unclear role of funders
- relevant institutional or organizational relationships
- relevant commercial, advocacy, political, or policy roles
- sensitive topic involving clinical care, public health, regulation, commercial products, or vulnerable populations
- conceptual ambiguity
- possible overstatement of conclusions
- possible mismatch between measurement and interpretation
- possible mismatch between statistical results and stated conclusions

## Important Limits

Do not accuse authors of misconduct.

Do not infer undisclosed conflicts of interest without evidence.

Do not rely on AI output alone.

AI should only identify candidate articles for human review.

A human reviewer must read the original article, disclosure statement, funding statement, author affiliations, and relevant public information before any letter, journal inquiry, institutional communication, or public comment is prepared.

## Human Review Reminder

For each flagged article, the human reviewer should ask:

- What does the article actually claim?
- What concept is being studied?
- How was the concept measured?
- Does the interpretation exceed the study design or data?
- Are conflicts of interest, funding, affiliations, and institutional relationships disclosed clearly enough for readers?
- Would a reasonable reader need additional information to interpret the article responsibly?

## Final Output

End with:

1. A short summary of the number of articles found.
2. A list of articles that may deserve human review.
3. A brief explanation of why each article was flagged.
4. A reminder that all flagged items require human verification.

## Suggested Closing Sentence

The items listed above are not findings of misconduct. They are candidate articles for careful human review as part of responsible post-publication peer review.
