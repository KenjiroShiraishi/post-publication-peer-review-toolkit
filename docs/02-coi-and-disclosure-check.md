# Conflict of Interest and Disclosure Check

This document provides a practical checklist for reviewing conflict of interest, funding, affiliation, and institutional relationship disclosures in published academic articles.

The goal is not to accuse authors of misconduct, but to help readers assess whether the published disclosure statements provide enough information for readers to interpret the article responsibly.

## Core Question

Would a reasonable reader need additional information about the authors’ financial, institutional, political, advocacy, or professional relationships in order to interpret the article?

## 2. Disclosure Statements

Check whether the article includes:

- Conflict of interest statement
- Funding statement
- Author affiliation statement
- Author contribution statement
- Ethics approval statement
- Data availability statement
- AI use disclosure, if relevant

## 3. Financial Relationships

Check whether any of the following relationships may be relevant to the article topic:

- Salary or employment
- Company ownership
- Shareholding
- Advisory roles
- Consulting fees
- Speaker fees
- Honoraria
- Patents or intellectual property
- Product sales
- Commercial sponsorship
- Industry-funded research

## 4. Institutional and Organizational Relationships

Check whether the authors have relevant relationships with:

- Non-profit organizations
- Advocacy groups
- Professional societies
- Patient organizations
- Policy groups
- Research institutes
- Government committees
- Task forces
- Regulatory bodies
- Commercially connected organizations

## 5. Non-Financial Relationships

Some relationships may be relevant even when no direct personal payment is involved.

Check whether the article topic may be affected by:

- Advocacy roles
- Public campaigning
- Political office
- Government advisory roles
- Leadership in professional organizations
- Public communication activities
- Institutional commitments
- Prior public positions on the topic

## 6. Funding Transparency

Ask:

- Who funded the study?
- Was the funding source clearly stated?
- Did the funder have any role in study design, data collection, analysis, interpretation, manuscript preparation, or publication decision?
- Are grant numbers provided?
- Are institutional or organizational sponsors clearly separated from personal financial relationships?

## 7. Reader-Relevance Test

Use the following test:

Would a reasonable reader want to know this relationship when interpreting the article?

Classify each potential issue:

- Not relevant
- Possibly relevant
- Clearly relevant
- Unclear and requires human review

## 8. Action Level

After completing the check, classify the article:

- No concern
- Minor disclosure clarification
- Needs human review
- Potential letter to the editor
- Potential formal journal inquiry
- No action without further evidence

## 9. Important Limits

This checklist does not determine misconduct.

Incomplete disclosure may result from oversight, journal form limitations, misunderstanding, or editorial handling. Readers should avoid personal accusations.

When a concern remains after careful human review, it should be addressed through appropriate scholarly channels, such as a letter to the editor, journal correspondence, or a formal inquiry to the editorial office.

## 10. Suggested Output

A short screening note may include:

- What was checked
- What disclosure statements were found
- What appears unclear
- Why the issue may matter for readers
- Whether further human review is needed
- Whether a letter or journal inquiry may be appropriate
