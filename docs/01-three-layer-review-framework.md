# Three-Layer Review Framework

This document provides a three-layer framework for responsible post-publication peer review.

The goal is not to accuse authors, but to help readers identify whether a published article contains conceptual ambiguity, interpretive overstatement, or statistical inference problems that may deserve scholarly discussion.

## Layer 1: Conceptual Mismatch

Ask whether the central concept is clearly defined and appropriately operationalized.

Key questions:

- What is the main concept being studied?
- Is the concept clearly defined?
- Is the measurement actually measuring the concept?
- Is a screening result treated as a diagnosis?
- Is a proxy variable treated as the phenomenon itself?
- Is a complex clinical, social, or ethical concept reduced too quickly to a single score?

## Layer 2: Meaning and Interpretation Mismatch

Ask whether the authors’ interpretation exceeds what the study can reasonably support.

Key questions:

- Do the conclusions match the study design?
- Is association interpreted as causation?
- Is patient-reported improvement treated as proof of mechanism?
- Is clinical meaning overstated from limited data?
- Are policy or practice recommendations stronger than the evidence allows?
- Are social, ethical, or cultural meanings added without sufficient support?

## Layer 3: Statistical Inference Mismatch

Ask whether the statistical analysis supports the stated claims.

Key questions:

- Is the sample size appropriate for the claim?
- Are confidence intervals, effect sizes, and uncertainty adequately discussed?
- Are subgroup analyses exploratory or confirmatory?
- Are multiple comparisons handled appropriately?
- Is statistical significance treated as clinical importance?
- Are noninferiority, equivalence, or superiority claims aligned with the design?
- Does the study infer causality from cross-sectional or observational data?

## Output of the Review

After completing the three-layer review, classify the concern:

- No substantial concern
- Minor interpretive issue
- Conceptual clarification needed
- Statistical interpretation concern
- Potential letter to the editor
- Potential formal journal inquiry
